Manache Ganpati of Pune

Lord Ganesha who is considered to be the God of wisdom and prosperity has been worshipped right from the Vedic times. Every religious ceremony is performed even today with invoking his blessings at the very beginning. Pune being the birthplace of Ganesh Festival, now celebrated all over India, has its own share of rich history. The city has the five Manache Ganpati — The respected and honored Ganpati idols at different places.

Tulshibaug Ganpati

Tulshibaug Ganpati is the 4th respected Ganpati in Pune. It was first established in 1901. This mandal holds the honor of establishing the first Glass Fiber statue since 1975. It is located in the heart and the most crowded part of the city.

Kasba Ganpati 

Kasba Ganpati is the presiding deity also known as the gramadevata of Pune. Kasba Ganpati originated during Shivaji Maharaj era which was found near the house of Vinayak Thakar, who was residing close to the residence of Shivaji’s Mother Masaheb Jijabai Bhosale.

Tambdi Jogeshwari 

Tambdi Jogeshwari is another peculiar spot to visit which holds the significance of being the oldest one in Pune. It is a Temple of Goddess Durga which is also regarded and determined as the patron deity of Pune city. The Ganpati idol is immersed every year at the end of Ganesh Festival and re-established again in the successive year.
